This is not an official church, at least not yet. "Churches" Defined | Internal Revenue Service lists various attributes of a church developed by the IRS. There is much to be done and this is a beginning. I am not sure what I will do here. I will start by suggesting some possibilities. I do not claim to be an expert on anything, I just want to get something started and I hope others will help.

Traditional churches place strong emphasis on worshiping God. I assume it is very important to God that we help each other. The emphasis of this church is on each other. This church is intended to be more of an enhancement of other religions than to exclude and/or "compete with" other religions. In other words, members are free to be believers in other religions.

One problem is society is that it is easy and extremely tempting to be critical. What the world needs is solutions, not criticisms. It is easy to complain and criticize but not so easy to provide practical solutions to problems. One thing in particular that would be easy to do is to criticize religions. I want to avoid doing that and instead simply provide good solutions to problems.

The emphasis is on providing guidance in the area of interpersonal communication and other things that people would call psychological. One way of describing the Church of People is to say it is for amateur psychologists. The emphasis is on caring about others at the emotional level. Hopefully this can be a way for people to gather over the internet with an emphasis on helping people emotionally in ways that are rare in the world.

Very many people don't know how to solve problems, at least not emotional problems. They don't know how to discuss problems and work things out. This is the type of thing I think should be important for a church.

Conflict Resolution

Many people don't know how to discuss problems and resolve them. For those people, it seems that when I do try to discuss problems they think it is just another person complaining and criticizing. I want to create a way for people to discuss the mature way to discuss problems and resolve them.

Constructive and Destructive Criticism

I think we have all heard the term Constructive Criticism. What is the opposite of Constructive Criticism? What is Destructive Criticism? I think one important difference is that Constructive Criticism is intended to begin a conversation and Destructive Criticism is intended to end a conversation. If someone has a criticism then the healthy thing to do is to try to solve the problem. Criticizing it and then leaving is not helping.

Bickering

We see it often, at least in television. People bickering about something. Nearly always, the resolution is to say something like:

Stop it; just stop your immature bickering. You are behaving like children.

Is bickering immature? Is telling people that it is immature the mature thing to do? Is there nothing better to do than saying it is immature and to never do it?

I think the mature thing to do is to understand what the problem is. Understand how it started and what started it. I think that quite often, one person is mature and the other is not. In those cases, just telling them to stop can reward the immature person. Some people just complain/bicker until the problem goes away and then telling both sides to stop just makes the problem go away so that the immature person knows that it is how to behave. Understanding can be difficult to do; it takes time and emotions, but it is the mature thing to do.

Addiction and Repression

Which comes first, addiction or repression? People become addicted to various things. For some things, such as alcohol and tobacco, the addiction is medical, at least partially. For other things, such as gambling, it is entirely psychological. I think that quite often, telling someone they can't have something just makes them want it more. An understanding and supportive person would not try to make someone change just by telling them they can't have it.
